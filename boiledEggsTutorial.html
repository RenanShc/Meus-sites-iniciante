Perfect Boiled Eggs

A foolproof method for silky, creamy boiled eggs every single time. Whether you love a runny yolk or a firm center, this guide has you covered.

Ingredients

  • 4 to 6 large eggs (preferably 7–10 days old for easier peeling)
  • Cold water (enough to cover eggs by at least 1 inch)
  • Ice cubes for the ice bath
  • A pinch of flaky sea salt (for serving)

Instructions

  1. Place the eggs in a single layer at the bottom of a medium saucepan. Avoid stacking them.
  2. Pour in cold water until the eggs are submerged by at least one inch.
  3. Bring the water to a rolling boil over high heat, then immediately remove the pan from the burner and cover it with a tight-fitting lid.
  4. Let the eggs sit in the hot water: 4 minutes for soft-boiled, 6 minutes for jammy, 9 minutes for medium, or 12 minutes for hard-boiled.
  5. While the eggs cook, fill a large bowl with cold water and plenty of ice cubes.
  6. When the timer ends, transfer the eggs to the ice bath with a slotted spoon and cool for at least 5 minutes.
  7. Peel the eggs gently under cool running water — the shells should slide right off.
  8. Slice, season with salt and pepper, and enjoy immediately or store in the fridge.
